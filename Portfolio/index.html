Find Your Rep

My first group project for General Assembly is a Ruby application running on Sinatra and Sqlite. The application allows users to locate their Congressman or Senator by entering their address or choosing their state. There is also basic information regarding contacting your representative and their attendance history, or comittees they belong to.

Buy Chicago

My final solo project for General Assembly is a Node.js application using MongoDB and the Google maps Javascript API. The purpose of the website is to make it easier to find and get information on property owned by the City of Chicago. The current process involves downloading a vry unhelpful CSV listing of the properties, and then cross referencing with the cook county propert appraisers office. Buy Chicago brings all those resources together in one simple to use interface.

FAQs

Answers to Frequently Asked Questions

What is your work style?

Typically a client will approach with an idea for a project, my first step is to listen, by listening I gain an understanding of not only what the client wants but what they need. I have a very collaborative work style and with scheduled frequent checkpoints, and contious flow of feedback my clients find my style open, professional and collaborative.

Why did you decide to become a Web Developer?

The decision to become a Web Developer did not come lightly. After several years of various jobs from bartending, to customer service supervisor for an auto insurance company. I realized that they all had a few things in common, lack of passion on my part, and they were just jobs not a career ( for me anyway). Web Development is one of the few things that I would learn and experiment with for free, when I realized that I could get paid to play and create I was sold. I formally began my education and training with General Assembly in May of 201 and have never regretted making the choice.

What is your average project turnaround?

Time from initial client meeting to project delivery is based upon each individual project. The clearer the direction of the project is the quicker the turnaround will be.
